Visualizing Career and Peak WAR

  1. Hover over a curve to see who the player is
  2. Hover over a dot to see that player's wins above k war value at that value of k
  3. Click on a curve to see that player highlighted in the table (you may need to scroll down in the table)
  4. Click on a row in the table to see that player's curve highlighted
  5. Check the Hall of Fame box to see all hall of fame players (players not in hall of fame fade out)
  6. Select a position from the dropdown to see all players who play that position (select the first blank option to deselect all positions)
  7. Search for or choose a player in the text field to highlight his curve
  8. Refresh the page or click the Reset All button to clear all highlighting

What is WAR?

WAR stands for Wins above Replacement. It is used as a metric for determining the value that a player has to a team. The higher a player's WAR value is, the more valuable they are to the team for which they play. There are 3 different ways to calculate a player's WAR score. This project uses FanGraph's WAR
